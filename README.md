## Employee Attrition
 ### Problem Statement:
  The main aim of the project is to analyze employee data to identify the key factors contributing to attrition and provide actionable
  insights to improve employee retention. The analysis focuses on factors such as salary, overtime, tenure, and job satisfaction, and
  presents the findings through an interactive dashboard to support data-driven HR decision-making.

 ### Objective:
  •  Identify patterns and trends behind employee attrition.
  
  •  Analyze key drivers such as compensation, workload, and satisfaction.
  
  •  Build an interactive dashboard for business insights.
  
  •  Enable HR teams to make informed retention decisions.

 ### Tools and Technologies:
  •  Excel → Data cleaning and initial exploration.
  
  •  PostgreSQL (SQL) → Data analysis and querying.
  
  •  Power BI → Data visualization and dashboard creation.

 ### Data Workflow:
   ### 1.) Data Cleaning and Preprocessing:
  •  Removed irrelevant and constant columns.
  
  •  Ensured data consistency and quality.
  
  •  Handled outliers in key features like monthly income.
  
  •  Created derived fields such as tenure group.

   ### 2.) Data Analysis (SQL):
  Performed exploratory data analysis using SQL to uncover key insights:
  
  •  Attrition rate across departments and job roles.
  
  •  Salary comparison between current and former employees.
  
  •  Tenure-based attrition patterns.
  
  •  Impact of overtime on employee turnover.

   ### 3.) Data Visualization (Power BI):
  Designed a visually appealing and interactive dashboard to display key insights:
   ###  Key KPIs:
  •  Attrition Rate (%)
  
  •  Employees Left
  
  •  Average Monthly Income
  
  •  Job Satisfaction Score
  
  •  Promotion Gap
   ### Key Visuals:
   
  •  Attrition by Job Role
  
  •  Attrition Distribution
  
  •  Attrition by Tenure
  
  •  Attrition by Overtime
  
  •  Attrition by Salary Hike
  
  •  Attrition by Monthly Income

 ### Key Insights:
 
  •  Higher attrition observed among employees working overtime.
 
  •  Employees with lower salaries are more likely to leave.
 
  •  Majority of attrition occurs in early tenure (0–5 years).
 
  •  Lower job satisfaction is linked to higher attrition.
 
 ### Business Recommendations:
 
  •  Optimize workload to reduce excessive overtime.
  
  •  Review compensation structure for lower salary bands.
  
  •  Focus on employee engagement during early tenure.
  
  •  Improve job satisfaction through better work environment and policies.
